I am a Post Doctorate Research Associate in Atmospheric Sciences & Global Change, Pacific Northwest National Laboratory, with an interest in contributing to a deeper understanding of atmospheric chemistry and climate change.

Atmospheric air pollutants, such as aerosols and ozone, have adverse effects on human health, atmospheric visibility, crops, and plants. They have also made significant contributions to radiative forcing of climate. I employ earth system models and global chemical transport models to better understand:

(1) Interactions between aerosol, cloud, radiation, circulation and climate;

(2) Global and regional sources, sinks, transport, and radiative forcing of pollutants;

(3) Impacts of meteorology and emissions on regional air quality;

(4) Intensification of haze in China.
